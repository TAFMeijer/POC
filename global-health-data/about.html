About This Site

A personal hub for open global health data, tools, and resources.

Mission

Making Health Data More Accessible

Global health data is often locked behind complex databases, paywalled journals, or dense government reports. This site exists to change that — by providing clean, interactive tools and curated references that make health financing and systems data easy to explore for researchers, policymakers, analysts, and practitioners.

The focus is on low- and middle-income countries, particularly in Sub-Saharan Africa, where health data infrastructure is rapidly developing and good information can directly improve policy decisions.

Focus Areas

What This Site Covers

Health Financing

Government health budgets, expenditure flows, out-of-pocket spending, and donor financing trends across countries and time.

Health Information Systems

Country-level data on HMIS maturity, DHIS2 deployments, data quality, and digital health infrastructure across Sub-Saharan Africa.

WHO / World Bank Data

Visualizations and query tools built on top of authoritative international sources: GHED, GBD, GHO, and World Bank open data APIs.

Open Research

Curated links to landmark papers, WHO reports, and freely available datasets relevant to global health policy and systems strengthening.

Background

Why These Tools Exist

Working in global health means spending a lot of time wrangling spreadsheets, navigating fragmented databases, and repeating the same analyses. The tools on this site started as personal utilities — built to answer specific questions faster — and are now shared openly in case they're useful to others.

The emphasis is on practical, low-friction access: no sign-ups, no paywalls. Just open the tool, ask a question, get an answer.
